# Behavioral Pivot — Final Consistency Pass (experience, not code)

*The test: could someone spend a week using Synapse for studying / building a business / exercising / breaking a habit / planning their future without once feeling they'd opened a repurposed health app? Honest verdict below. Engine untouched; experiences broadened; new domains captured qualitatively.*

## The honest verdict

**Surface & behavior: yes — it no longer feels like a health app.** The frame a new user meets — landing, onboarding, the three surfaces, the conversation, the daily "what did you move forward," roles that actually change shape, focus-from-language — is person-centered across work, study, fitness, habits, and planning. A week of using it for studying or a business would feel native in the parts that matter most day to day (Talk, Focus, progress reflection, weekly review, You).

**Underneath: the *quantitative evidence* is still health-shaped, and that's the one place the seam shows.** The frozen engine only measures health signals (sleep, energy, stress, mood, focus, reaction time), so:
- the daily check-in's secondary section is still health sliders (now honestly framed as "one lens"),
- and any *numeric* discovery ("your focus runs a day behind your sleep") is health-metric based.

Non-health domains are now captured **qualitatively** — goals, aspiration, progress notes, coaching preferences — which the conversation and weekly review genuinely reason over. But Synapse can't yet produce a *quantitative* surprise about your study hours or deep-work streaks, because there's no non-health metric to correlate. That is the deferred "domain-agnostic metric engine," and I'm not faking it.

**So, against the completion criterion:** for someone using Synapse to study, build, train, break a habit, or plan — the experience is now person-centered and consistent in every workflow they touch daily. The remaining health-shaped residue is (a) the optional "how you're feeling" lens in the daily check-in and (b) the *type* of quantitative pattern the engine can surface. Both are honest one-lens artifacts of a frozen health-metric engine, not leftover product identity.

**What I did not do (and why):** I did not bolt a fake multi-domain metric layer onto the frozen engine to hide the seam — that would violate "preserve the engine" and would overclaim. The clean way to close the last gap is the deferred domain-metric work, which is a real engine investment, not a behavioral polish.

**Would I call the behavioral pivot complete?** For the MVP thesis — *does it feel like an AI that understands your life, not a health tracker* — **yes at the experience layer.** The one truthful caveat to carry into beta: the *quantitative* intelligence is still health-domain until the metric engine is broadened. Everything a user does and reads is now person-first; what the engine can still only *measure* is health.

*tsc passes. Engine, memory, safety untouched.*
